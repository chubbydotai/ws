Mission

Help feed, house, and self-actualize humans.

What problem exists?

There are somewhere between 750,000 to 1 million homeless humans in the USA on a given night.

According to Realtor.com, the USA market was short 4 million homes in 2024.

There has been a major push for domestic USA manufacturing as the AI / humanoid robotics race with China accelerates.

There are roughly 2 billion humans or 1/4 of the planet without adequate access to toilets and clean water.

There will be an additional 2 billion people on the planet within the next 25 years.

AI investment is accelerating end-to-end, creating power limited data centers.

Data center and manufacturing capacity align with more people and housing developments.

There are roughly as many school districts as municipalities, yet half as many post-secondary institutions.

Lack of post-secondary education facilities are limiting for human intelligence in a similar way that lack of electricity generation facilities is limiting for artificial intelligence. There are nearly twice as many utility scale power generating facilities as Title IV post-secondary institutions, with a major push to build more. No such push for higher education exists at such scale.

Without planning, communities sparked by economic development can have disastrous consequences to quality of life for all involved.

We need to build manufacturing and data center capacity for AI and humanoids to be of the most value to humans. Locality of that space matters.

Simplified: We need AI driven factories, higher education, and town infrastructure decentralized around the USA and abroad.

This is how we build millions of homes, while also planning out the neighborhoods for a high quality of life.

Building a distributed and decentralized supply chain infrastructure for food, housing, data centers, electronics, robotics, and everything else for human needs to be met.

This is simple, turn land into buildings and infrastructure. We are building housing for happier humans and facilities for businesses to thrive.

AI abundance won't come overnight, it is a long-term collaboration of humans and machines, as it has been for the entire industrial revolution.

We are building small manufacturing data centers that seed into building 100-2,000 acre sidekick towns dedicated to manufacturing, education, and affordable quality housing. What are our core values?

Our mission is simplified into meeting human needs based on theories summed up by Maslow's Hierarchy and Tony Robbins 6 Human Needs.

Maslow's Hierarchy

Physiological: air, water, food, shelter, sleep, clothing, reproduction.

Safety: personal security, employment, resources, health, prosperity.

Love and belonging: friendship, intimacy, family, sense of connection.

Esteem: respect, self-esteem, status, recognition, strength, freedom.

Self-actualization: desire to become the most that one can be.

These are the core values that we look to for inspiration to empower humans to reach self-actualization. Each value is followed by the Tony Robbins Human Need.

Love Harder: Love/Connection
